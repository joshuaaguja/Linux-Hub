LINUX OS

Linux is a family of open-source and Unix-like operating systems based on the Linux kernel, an operating system kernel first created by Linus Torvalds in 1991. The Linux kernel
serves as the core component of a Linux operating system and provides low-level system services to manage hardware, memory, process scheduling, and other essential functions.

Ubuntu Distro

Ubuntu is one of the most known and most used distros of Linux. It was developed in the 1990s in South Africa

Story of Linus Torvalds

Torvalds was the inventor of the Linux operating system as a way to create an open-source version of Unix

Unix OS and its Descendants

Unix is a family of multitasking, multiuser computer operating systems that were originally developed in the 1960s and 1970s.
